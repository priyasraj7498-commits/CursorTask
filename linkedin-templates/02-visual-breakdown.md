# Template 2: The Visual Breakdown Post

## Post Copy:
Most B2B slide decks fail before the prospect even reads the first bullet point. 

Why? Because they violate the 60-30-10 rule of visual attention. 

They jam 80% text onto a dark background and expect a busy executive to squint through a pitch. 

Here is how we redesigned our core sales deck to close $140k in ARR last month (Swipe through the graphic below):

*   **Slide 1: The Villain.** 60% negative space, a bright red headline highlighting the number one problem they face today. 
*   **Slide 2: The Core Metric.** A clean, single data chart comparing their current reality vs. their potential future state.
*   **Slide 3: The Roadmap.** A three-step linear sequence showing exactly how we handle the onboarding process.

When you design for scannability, you lower the cognitive friction required to make a buying decision.
